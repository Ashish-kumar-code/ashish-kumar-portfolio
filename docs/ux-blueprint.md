# Product Overview

The portfolio is a premium personal website designed to showcase Ashish Kumar's technical expertise, projects, internships, and problem-solving abilities to recruiters, engineering managers, and hiring teams.

Rather than functioning as a simple resume website, the portfolio serves as an interactive product that demonstrates frontend engineering, backend understanding, UI/UX thinking, and software architecture.

The website focuses on credibility, clarity, and measurable impact while maintaining excellent performance, accessibility, and responsiveness.

---

## Primary Goal

Help recruiters understand within 30 seconds:

• Who I am

• What I build

• Technologies I use

• Projects I've developed

• Experience I have

• Why I'm worth interviewing

---

## Secondary Goals

• Demonstrate frontend development skills

• Showcase backend engineering knowledge

• Present real-world projects through detailed case studies

• Highlight GitHub activity

• Provide downloadable resumes

• Offer an easy way to contact me

---

## Success Metrics

The portfolio is considered successful if:

• Recruiters understand my profile within 30 seconds.

• Projects receive more attention than the resume.

• The website achieves excellent Lighthouse scores.

• The design feels modern, premium, and memorable.

• The portfolio is easy to maintain as new projects are added.

# Core Design Principles

Every design decision must follow these principles.

## 1. Clarity over Creativity

The recruiter should understand the website immediately.

## 2. Projects over Decorations

Projects are the main attraction.

Animations should support the content, not distract from it.

## 3. Consistency

Every section follows the same spacing, typography, and design language.

## 4. Performance First

Every animation must justify its existence.

No unnecessary JavaScript.

## 5. Accessibility

The website should be usable for everyone.

Keyboard navigation.

Proper contrast.

Semantic HTML.

## 6. Mobile First

Every component should work perfectly on phones before desktops.

## 7. Maintainability

Content should be editable without changing components.
